# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA / Testing

### Role Summary
QA Engineers ensure quality and validate that features meet acceptance criteria before release. They design test plans, execute tests, and work closely with developers to identify and resolve defects.

### Responsibilities
- Create test plans and test cases based on requirements
- Execute manual and automated tests
- Report and track defects with clear reproduction steps
- Validate fixes and regression test
- Collaborate with Product Managers and Developers on acceptance criteria

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality releases with minimal defects
- Reduce time to detect and resolve issues
- Improve test coverage and automation

### Typical Communication
- Bug reports and test results
- Daily standups and sprint reviews
- Test coverage reports and quality metrics

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers create intuitive, accessible, and user-centric interfaces. They conduct user research, design wireframes and prototypes, and ensure the product delivers an excellent user experience.

### Responsibilities
- Develop high-fidelity designs, wireframes, and interactive prototypes
- Conduct user research and usability testing to validate design decisions
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature requirements and user flows
- Work with Developers to ensure design implementation fidelity
- Iterate on feedback from QA, stakeholders, and user testing
- Maintain design systems and component libraries

### Goals
- Create user experiences that are intuitive and accessible
- Reduce user friction and increase engagement
- Ensure design consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User testing reports and research findings
- Design specs and handoff documentation via Figma or similar platforms
- Collaboration with Product Managers on roadmaps and feature prioritization

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on feature definition, user stories, and prioritization based on user needs
- **Developers**: Provide design specifications, review implementations, and support technical feasibility discussions
- **QA/Testing**: Partner on usability validation and visual regression testing
- **Business Analysts**: Align on user workflows and process improvements

---

## Tech Lead / Lead Engineer

### Role Summary
Tech Leads guide the engineering team on technical decisions, set coding standards, and ensure architectural integrity. They mentor team members and support the Project Manager in technical risk assessment and effort estimation.

### Responsibilities
- Oversee system architecture and technical direction
- Conduct code reviews and mentor engineers
- Define coding standards, best practices, and technical guidelines
- Support Project Managers in assessing technical risks and effort estimates
- Make architectural decisions and document technical design
- Lead technical discussions and resolve technical blockers

### Goals
- Maintain high code quality and architectural consistency
- Foster technical growth and knowledge sharing within the team
- Balance technical excellence with delivery timelines

### Typical Communication
- Code review comments and pull request feedback
- Technical design documents and architecture decision records (ADRs)
- Team meetings and technical planning sessions
- One-on-one mentoring sessions with developers

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Mentor, review code, and provide technical guidance and direction
- **Project Managers**: Advise on technical complexity, risks, and realistic timelines
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on technical feasibility and trade-offs for features
- **QA/Testing**: Support test strategy and automation infrastructure decisions

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical solutions by clarifying requirements, mapping processes, and translating business objectives into actionable user stories and acceptance criteria.

### Responsibilities
- Map current and target business processes
- Elicit and document business requirements
- Write detailed user stories with clear acceptance criteria
- Provide business impact analysis and ROI assessments
- Facilitate requirements workshops with stakeholders
- Validate solutions against business objectives

### Goals
- Ensure solutions address real business needs effectively
- Reduce requirement ambiguity and rework
- Maximize business value delivery

### Typical Communication
- Process maps and workflow diagrams
- User story documentation and acceptance criteria
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Business impact assessments and feasibility reports

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on roadmap prioritization and feature definition based on business value
- **Developers**: Clarify requirements and acceptance criteria, answer questions during implementation
- **Project Managers**: Support scope definition, dependency mapping, and change management
- **Support Engineers**: Gather insights from user issues to inform process improvements

---

## Support Engineer / Customer Success

### Role Summary
Support Engineers and Customer Success team members ensure user issues are addressed quickly and effectively. They serve as the voice of the customer, providing critical feedback that drives product improvements and feature prioritization.

### Responsibilities
- Respond to user queries and troubleshoot issues
- Log, triage, and escalate support tickets
- Communicate patterns, trends, or critical incidents to Product Managers and Engineering
- Update knowledge base and support documentation
- Track customer satisfaction and identify improvement opportunities
- Provide user training and onboarding assistance

### Goals
- Achieve fast resolution times and high customer satisfaction
- Reduce recurring issues through proactive identification
- Enable users to self-serve through clear documentation

### Typical Communication
- Support tickets and incident reports
- User feedback summaries and trend analysis
- Knowledge base articles and help documentation
- Regular sync meetings with Product and Engineering teams

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Share user feedback, feature requests, and pain points to inform roadmap
- **Developers**: Report bugs with clear reproduction steps and collaborate on fixes
- **QA/Testing**: Provide real-world usage scenarios and edge cases for testing
- **Business Analysts**: Offer insights into user workflows and common process challenges
